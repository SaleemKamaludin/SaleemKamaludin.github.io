# Assignment 8 — Final Exam Hints

---

## Key Skills to Master

- **Working with complex numbers (definitions and proofs)**
  - You must be comfortable using the general definition of complex numbers.
  - Be able to **prove or disprove statements** involving complex numbers.
  - This is especially important for:
    - **Question 2(b)(i) and (ii)**

---

- **Roots of complex numbers (De Moivre’s Theorem)**
  - This is a **guaranteed exam topic** (based on Question 4).
  - The power may not be \(7\); it could be:
    - A smaller integer
    - A fractional power

  - You must:
    1. Convert the complex number into polar form.
    2. Clearly find the **modulus** and **argument**.
    3. Apply De Moivre’s Theorem:

$$
z^{1/n} = r^{1/n} \left[\cos\left(\frac{\theta + 2\pi k}{n}\right) + i\sin\left(\frac{\theta + 2\pi k}{n}\right)\right], \quad k = 0,1,\dots,n-1.
$$

  - You must clearly show:
    - Each value of \(k\), starting from \(k=0\)
    - The full computation for **every root**

---

- **Sum and product of roots**
  - You must clearly compute the **sum** and **product** of the roots you find.
  - Do **not** simply state the final answer like 0 or -1.
  - You must show the working that leads to the result.

---

- **Argand diagram**
  - You must be able to draw a **clear and accurate Argand diagram**.
  - Requirements:
    - Label the real and imaginary axes
    - Plot **all roots correctly**
    - Label each root explicitly (in exact form, e.g., surd or trigonometric form)
  - Writing labels such as $z_1, z_2, \dots$ alone is **not sufficient** and no marks will be given.

---

## Important Questions to Review

- **Question 2(b)(i), (ii)** — Proofs involving complex numbers  
- **Question 4** — Roots of complex numbers and Argand diagram  

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Not converting complex numbers correctly into polar form.
- Failing to compute the modulus and argument clearly.
- Skipping steps when applying De Moivre’s Theorem.
- Forgetting to start with \(k=0\) when finding roots.
- Writing only the final sum/product without showing working.
- Poor or incomplete Argand diagrams (missing labels or roots).
