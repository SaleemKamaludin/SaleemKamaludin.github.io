# Assignment 3 — Final Exam Hints

---

## Key Skills to Master

- **Forming the equation of a plane**
  - You must be able to form the Cartesian equation of a plane from given points.
  - This is especially important in **Question 1 (e.g., part b)**.
  - Recall that a plane can be written as:

$$
ax + by + cz = d
$$

  - Be comfortable finding a **normal vector** and using it to construct the equation.

- **Working with equations of planes**
  - You should be comfortable manipulating plane equations.
  - This includes:
    - Substituting points
    - Rearranging equations
    - Performing basic algebra correctly
  - See **Question 3**.

- **Distance from a point**
  - You must know how to find:
    - Distance from a point to a line
    - Distance from a point to a plane
  - For a plane, use:

$$
\text{Distance}
=
\frac{|ax_0 + by_0 + cz_0 - d|}
{\sqrt{a^2 + b^2 + c^2}}.
$$

  - These ideas are central in **Question 4**.

- **Angles in 3D geometry**
  - You must be able to find angles between:
    - Two lines
    - Two planes
    - A line and a plane
  - Key ideas:
    - Line–line: use direction vectors
    - Plane–plane: use normal vectors
    - Line–plane: relate direction vector and normal vector
  - General form:

$$
\cos\theta
=
\frac{\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}}
{\|\mathbf{a}\|\|\mathbf{b}\|}.
$$

  - See **Question 5**.

## Important Questions to Review

- **Question 1** — Forming plane equations from points  
- **Question 3** — Working with plane equations  
- **Question 4** — Distances (point to line and point to plane)  
- **Question 5** — Angles between lines and planes  

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Not correctly finding or using the normal vector of a plane.
- Errors when substituting points into plane equations.
- Forgetting absolute values in distance formulas.
- Confusing which vectors to use when finding angles.
- Mixing up formulas for line–line, plane–plane, and line–plane angles.

Assignment 3 brings together geometry and algebra. Focus on understanding how equations of lines and planes relate to vectors, and practise interpreting results geometrically.
