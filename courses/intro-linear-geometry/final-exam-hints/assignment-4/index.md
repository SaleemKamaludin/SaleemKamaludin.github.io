# Assignment 4 — Final Exam Hints

---

## Key Skills to Master

- **Row reduction (Gaussian elimination)**
  - You must be able to row reduce **quickly, accurately, and systematically**.
  - This is essential for almost every question in this assignment.
  - Always aim to reach **row echelon form (REF)** or **reduced row echelon form (RREF)** efficiently.
  - See **Question 1**.

- **Systems with unknown parameters**
  - Be very careful when working with unknowns (parameters) during row reduction.
  - This is heavily tested in **Question 2**.
  - Key strategy:
    - Try to obtain **at least two pivot columns early**.
    - This makes it easier to:
      - Back substitute
      - Determine conditions on the unknowns
  - Always watch for values that make a pivot zero (these often lead to special cases).

- **Classifying solutions**
  - After row reducing, you must clearly identify the type of solution:
    - Unique solution
    - Infinitely many solutions
    - No solution
  - This is important in **Questions 3(a) and 3(b)**.

- **Solving vs describing**
  - Be careful with instructions:
    - If the question says **“solve”**, you must give the **complete solution set**.
    - If infinitely many solutions exist, express your answer in **parametric form**.

---

## Important Questions to Review

- **Question 1** — Row reduction techniques  
- **Question 2** — Systems with parameters (very important)  
- **Question 3(a), 3(b)** — Classifying and solving systems  

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Making arithmetic errors during row operations.
- Not recognizing when a pivot becomes zero due to a parameter.
- Failing to consider special cases when parameters take certain values.
- Not stating the type of solution (unique, infinite, or none).
- Stopping at row echelon form without fully solving when required.
- Giving an incomplete solution when the question says “solve”.
