# Assignment 1 — Final Exam Hints

---

## Key Skills to Master

- **Projection of one vector onto another**
  - You should know how to project one vector onto another vector.
  - This is especially important for **Assignment 1, Question 1(b)**.
  - The projection of $\mathbf{u}$ onto $\mathbf{v}$ is:

$$
\operatorname{proj}_{\mathbf{v}}(\mathbf{u})
=
\frac{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}}{\|\mathbf{v}\|^2}\mathbf{v}.
$$

  - Remember that the vector being projected is $\mathbf{u}$, while the vector being projected onto is $\mathbf{v}$.

- **Using vector methods carefully**
  - Be comfortable applying dot products, magnitudes, and projections in geometric problems.
  - Always define your vectors clearly before carrying out calculations.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Mixing up the vector being projected with the vector being projected onto.
- Forgetting that the denominator is $\|\mathbf{v}\|^2$, not $\|\mathbf{u}\|^2$.
- Forgetting to multiply by the vector $\mathbf{v}$ at the end of the projection formula.
- Treating the projection as only a scalar instead of a vector.
