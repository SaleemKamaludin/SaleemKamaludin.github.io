# Assignment 9 — Final Exam Hints

---

## Key Skills to Master

- **Using the Binomial Theorem with complex numbers**
  - You must be able to expand expressions involving complex numbers using the binomial theorem.
  - This is used to derive identities for multiple angles of sine and cosine.

---

- Start with:
$$
z = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta.
$$

  - Then:
$$
z^n = \cos(n\theta) + i\sin(n\theta).
$$

  - Also recall:
$$
z^{-1} = \cos\theta - i\sin\theta.
$$

---

- **Extracting sine and cosine identities**
  - You must clearly state and use:

$$
\cos(n\theta) = \frac{z^n + z^{-n}}{2}
$$

$$
\sin(n\theta) = \frac{z^n - z^{-n}}{2i}.
$$

  - These must be written **before beginning your expansion**.

---

- **Separating real and imaginary parts**
  - After expanding using the binomial theorem:
    - The **real part** corresponds to $\cos(n\theta)$
    - The **imaginary part** corresponds to $\sin(n\theta)$

  - You must clearly state:
    - Which terms are real
    - Which terms are imaginary

---

## Important Skills to Demonstrate

- Correct binomial expansion of complex expressions  
- Clear identification of real and imaginary parts  
- Proper derivation of multiple-angle identities  

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Not stating the identities before starting the solution.
- Mixing up the formulas for sine and cosine.
- Failing to separate real and imaginary parts clearly.
- Algebra errors when applying the binomial theorem.
- Skipping steps in the expansion.
