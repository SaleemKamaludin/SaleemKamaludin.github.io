# Assignment 2 — Final Exam Hints

---

## Key Skills to Master

- **Forms of a line in 3D**
  - You must know all standard forms of a line:
    - Vector form
    - Parametric form
    - Symmetric form
  - Be able to **convert between these forms easily**.
  - Understand how lines relate to each other:
    - Parallel lines
    - Intersecting lines
    - Skew lines

- **Classifying lines**
  - You should be able to determine whether two lines are:
    - Parallel
    - Intersecting
    - Skew
  - Always compare **direction vectors** and check for **common solutions**.

- **Shortest distance between lines**
  - This is a key exam topic.
  - For skew lines, you must be able to find the shortest distance.
  - The formula for the shortest distance between two skew lines is:

$$
\text{Distance}
=
\frac{|(\mathbf{a}_2 - \mathbf{a}_1)\cdot(\mathbf{d}_1 \times \mathbf{d}_2)|}
{\|\mathbf{d}_1 \times \mathbf{d}_2\|}.
$$

  - Be sure you understand:
    - Where each vector comes from
    - Why the cross product is used

- **Angle between lines**
  - After determining the relationship between lines, you must be able to find the angle between them.
  - The angle between two lines is the angle between their direction vectors:

$$
\cos\theta
=
\frac{\mathbf{d}_1 \cdot \mathbf{d}_2}
{\|\mathbf{d}_1\|\|\mathbf{d}_2\|}.
$$

## Important Questions to Review

- **Question 1** — All forms of a line and conversions
- **Question 2** — Classifying lines and shortest distance
- **Question 3 and 4** — Full coverage (classification + angles between lines)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing skew lines with parallel lines.
- Forgetting to check whether equations have a common solution before concluding lines intersect.
- Errors when computing cross products in the distance formula.
- Not taking the absolute value in the distance formula.
- Using incorrect vectors when calculating angles between lines.

Assignment 2 is heavily tested. Focus on understanding how lines behave in 3D, how to classify them, and how to compute both distances and angles accurately.
