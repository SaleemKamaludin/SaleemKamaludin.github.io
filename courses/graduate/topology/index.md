# MATH 6620 — Topology

---

## Course Description

Topology is the study of spaces and sets and can be thought of as an extension of geometry. It investigates both the local and global structure of spaces and examines properties that remain unchanged under continuous transformations.
General Topology (or Point-Set Topology) is founded on set theory and provides a modern framework for understanding continuity, connectedness, compactness, and convergence. The motivation behind topology is that many geometric problems depend not on the exact shape of an object, but rather on how the object is structured or connected.
This course provides an overview of the central ideas of General Topology and illustrates how topology serves as a unifying theme across several branches of mathematics. Students are introduced to important concepts such as topological spaces, topological vector spaces, connectedness, compactness, and metric spaces.
